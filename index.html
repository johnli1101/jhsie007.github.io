Projects
Robotics: Micromouse

Micromouse is a small robot programmed with searching algorithms, its objective is to solve any maze at some optimal speed.

The mouse is constructed by a customized PCB, IR sensors/emitters, and DC motors. The design of the mouse plays a significant role in optimizing the physical performance of the robot.

University of California Micromouse Competition: 3rd Place Winner - $400 (2015-2016)
Robot Programmer/Designer

UCR Research: Solar (Embedded Systems)

Dr. Kawai Tam leads this research project at University of California, Riverside. It is an energy conservation system, a smart home design striving to improve the current houshold technology.

Creating an embedded system to distribute solar energy, I created the prototype of this project, which I named it Solar. The program contains High-Level State Machines that operates with temperature/humidity sensors, inline-fans, water heaters, and more.

University of Calfornia, Riverside: EAP Team Award (2014-2016)
Lead Computer Engineer (2015-Present)
Operating Systems: Mutex, Threads, and Scheduler

Operating systems is perhaps one of the most intriguing topics in the CS field since it has the potential to improve a computer's performance significantly.

In this project, we explore the power of threads and its protections (mutex). We also discover some clever algorithms that supports the scheduler when handling multiple processes. The project becomes a great learning opportunity to delve into the world of operating systems.
